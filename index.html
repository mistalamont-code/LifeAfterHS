The Story Behind the Book

From the Lower East Side of Erie
to Entrepreneur, Community Leader & Executive

Growing up on the Lower East Side of Erie, Corey didn’t have a guidebook. No one sat him down and explained how credit works, how to build a career, or how one decision at 17 could shape the next 20 years. He learned by living it—over 16 years at UPS training people who got promoted ahead of him, starting a music mentoring program with 10–15 kids, wearing suits to events years before he had a “professional” job that required them.

When he became a father of five, he realized the advice he’d pieced together over a lifetime needed to be written down. Not just for his own children, but for every young person growing up in communities where that guidance doesn’t exist—where the gap between potential and opportunity is filled with silence.

“Nobody believed in me before I believed in myself. That’s just how it works. You have to see it before anyone else sees it.”

He never planned to become the COO of the Erie Downtown Development Corporation. He didn’t apply for the job. But because he’d built relationships, shown up consistently, and done good work—someone thought of him when the opportunity came. Every step was a decision. Every decision compounded. You are the sum of your decisions—and this book exists so the next generation doesn’t have to figure that out alone.

About the Author

Corey L. Cook

Community leader, entrepreneur, and mentor dedicated to creating opportunities for the next generation. As the Chief Operating Officer for the Erie Downtown Development Corporation (EDDC), he works every day to help revitalize his hometown of Erie, Pennsylvania.

Corey’s story is proof that where you start doesn’t determine where you finish. After graduating high school at age 20 and working for over 16 years at UPS, he committed himself to lifelong learning and service. He founded Lifethrumusic, a nonprofit mentoring program for youth, and serves as CEO of Cook Media LLC.

His work has been featured on Good Morning America, and he has served on state commissions under Pennsylvania Governors Tom Wolf and Josh Shapiro. In 2024, his civic leadership led to an invitation to meet with Vice President Kamala Harris to discuss the future of his community.

Beyond his work, Corey considers his most important role to be at home. He resides in Erie with his wife, Latasha, and their five children: Daevante’, Darrian, Cameron, Jordan, and Neriah.
